Abstrakt:
We propose a novel scientific framework for constructing a dynamic, frequency‑based map of the universe. Instead of starting from matter or spacetime, we take fundamental frequencies – physical, mathematical, and possibly intentional – as the primitive building blocks. These frequencies self‑organise, resonate, and gradually condense into fractal structures that mirror observed cosmic features (galaxy distribution, cosmic web, CMB fluctuations). The map is not static; it evolves according to a condensation equation ρ(ν, t) = ρ₀(ν) · f(t), where f(t) describes the growth of coherence and structure. The framework is compatible with string theory, inflationary cosmology, and fractal cosmology, and it suggests novel ways to integrate consciousness as an observational parameter (Third Observer). We outline a pilot implementation using open data (SDSS, Planck) and invite replication, critique, and collaboration.

1. Introduction
Current cosmological maps are built from matter distributions, light, and gravity. However, theoretical physics hints that frequency (vibration, resonance) may be more fundamental. In string theory, different particle species correspond to different vibrational modes. In quantum field theory, energy is proportional to frequency. In fractal cosmology, structures repeat across scales – a property naturally generated by frequency‑based, self‑similar rules. We propose a project that deliberately constructs a universe map from a basis set of frequencies and then simulates their condensation into a spatial/temporal structure.

2. Core Definitions

· Fundamental frequencies {ν_i}: can be derived from physical constants (Planck frequency, atomic transitions), mathematical constants (π, e, φ), or even intentionally chosen resonant sets (e.g., 528 Hz, 432 Hz).
· Condensation ρ(ν, t): a function that increases the “weight” or connectivity of a frequency node over time, representing its contribution to the emerging structure.
· Fractal topology: the rule that each frequency node can spawn sub‑nodes at higher or lower frequencies, replicating self‑similarity.

3. Mathematical Framework
Let ρ₀(ν) be the initial spectral density. We propose the dynamic equation:
∂ρ/∂t = α ρ (1 – ρ/ρ_max) + β ∇²ρ
where the first term is logistic growth (condensation) and the second term represents diffusive coupling between nearby frequencies. This is a reaction‑diffusion system on frequency space, known to produce Turing patterns – in our case, fractal cosmic webs.
